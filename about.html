About Braillino

Making
the world
readable.

Braillino is an Arduino-powered Braille e-reader built by students, designed to make digital text accessible to everyone — through touch.

Our Story

A classmate's story changed everything

During the pandemic, students were deprived of the liberty to socialize and experience the outside world. However, technology shed light on these dark times. Many relied on technology, social media, and other online activities to stay connected with others and tend to their academic responsibilities.

Although effective, it left a lasting impact on their eyesight. A classmate of ours shared his personal experience. Throughout his high school years, he noticed his vision was gradually worsening. From minor inconveniences such as having to reread food packaging labels, to completely losing the ability to read without prescription glasses.

As he simply put it, "I could not read without it, and I don't know what I would do without it." Our subjects, namely, Practical Research, Capstone, and Inquiries, Immersion, and Investigation has provided us an opportunity to make a difference.

"I could not read without it, and I don't know what I would do without it."
— A classmate, on losing his vision through screens
