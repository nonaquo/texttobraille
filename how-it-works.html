How It Works

Four steps
to touch
your words.

From uploading the Arduino sketch to feeling Braille patterns on your fingertips — here's exactly how Braillino works.

1
Step One

Upload the Arduino Code

Open the Arduino IDE on your computer, paste in the Braillino .ino sketch, and configure your board settings before uploading.

  • Open Arduino IDE on your computer
  • Copy and paste the provided .ino code
  • Select your board (Tools → Board)
  • Select port (Tools → Port)
  • Click upload and wait for "Done uploading"
2
Step Two

Connect Your Device

Keep your Arduino plugged in via USB, then connect the web app to your device using Chrome or Edge's built-in Web Serial API.

  • Keep Arduino connected via USB
  • Open Braillino in Chrome or Edge
  • Click "Connect Device" button
  • Select Arduino from popup
  • Wait for "Connected" status
3
Step Three

Type Your Message

With your device connected, type any word or sentence. Text is automatically uppercased and displayed as Braille Grade 1.

  • Type any word or sentence (A–Z, spaces)
  • Maximum 100 characters per message
  • Text auto-converts to uppercase
  • Press Enter or click "Send to Braille"
4
Step Four

Experience Braille

Watch and feel the Arduino servo motors move in real time, forming each Braille letter under your fingertips, one character at a time.

  • Servos move to form each letter
  • Each character displays for 4 seconds
  • Short pause between repeated characters
  • Enter new message to continue reading
01

Hardware

Arduino Uno or Nano with 2 servo motors connected to pins 9 and 10

02

Software

Arduino IDE for uploading the Braillino sketch to your board

03

Browser

Chrome or Edge — required for Web Serial API support
